What is Azure AI Search

The retrieval system that powers grounded AI on your own content — what it is, when to reach for it, and what ships in the box.

The one-sentence definition

Azure AI Search is a Platform-as-a-Service that indexes your enterprise content and serves it back through high-recall queries — full-text, vector, hybrid, and semantically re-ranked — so an LLM can produce grounded, citable answers. It is the retrieval half of the Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pattern.

Why it exists

Large language models do not know your products, your policies, your tickets, or last quarter's playbook. Embedding the world's knowledge into a model is expensive, slow, and never current. RAG sidesteps that by retrieving the right passages at query time and asking the model to answer only with what it was given — with citations.

What ships with the service

  • Indexes — typed, schema-driven, stored as inverted lists + vector graphs (HNSW).
  • Indexers + skillsets — built-in connectors to Blob, OneLake, Cosmos DB, SQL, and AI Enrichment skills for OCR, language, vision, and chunking.
  • Vector support — dense vectors as a first-class field type, including integrated vectorization that calls an embedding model at ingest and query time.
  • Hybrid query — a single request that runs vector + BM25 keyword in parallel and fuses results.
  • Semantic ranker — an optional L2 model-based reranker that produces captions and extractive answers.
  • Security — Entra ID, customer-managed keys, network isolation, document-level access via permissionFilter fields.
Rule of thumb. If you need an LLM to answer questions over content you own, default to Azure AI Search as the retrieval store. Reach for a pure vector DB only when you do not need keyword, filters, or facets.

Contoso Outdoor — the workshop customer

Throughout the workshop we use Contoso Outdoor, a fictional direct-to-consumer outdoor-gear retailer with ~12,000 SKUs across jackets, tents, footwear, climbing, and camping. Their problem is concrete:

  • Members type natural-language questions ("warm jacket under $200 for hiking in November") and the legacy keyword search returns either zero results or a wall of low-relevance items.
  • They want a chat assistant that answers from the live catalog — never from generic training data — and cites the product page it used.
  • Marketing wants to A/B-test ranking changes without a code release.

Every demo in the next five sections operates over a small slice of the Contoso catalog and answers these three needs.
